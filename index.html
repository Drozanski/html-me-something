My Artwork

Summary

Art is a big part of my life. Ever since I was 4 years old, art just stuck with me and came naturally. I'm still learning, and i'm definetly not the best, but it's one of my ways that I can escape from everything and feel total nirvana. On this page, I'll show you some of my favorite pieces that i've done and give you a quick explanation on my inspiration and my feelings when I worked on them!

"Tangled" Drawing

Out of all Disney movies, "Tangled" is my favorite! Everything from the story, to the voice actor performances, and the animation, I simply love it! I remember sitting in my room, listening to the soundtrack for the movie, and then my sudden urge to draw came out. So, I decided to draw Flynn and Rapunzel together. I tend to draw a single character, rarely multiples in a piece, but to this day, I love this drawing solely based on the way they are looking at each other.

Superman Drawing

Now, this piece is an early one. I think I was a freshmen in High School(2009) when I did this one. It's not an original, I used a photo reference, but Im still proud of it. If I remember correctly, this drawing took me 3 days to complete. I'm not even much of a Superman fan(go team Batman!), but when I came across this photo, it just sparked something inside of saying "I need to draw this now!"e The hardest part were the hands, I still struggle to this day with those damn things!

80's Neon Inspired Drawing

This drawing is actually pretty recent. I drew this back in May of this year(2017), and it took me about an hour to finish. Most of my drawing to be honest only take me anywhere from an hour to 6 ours. I have a pretty messy/unfinished style. So with this one, I was Inspired by a movie called "Kung Fury"e which is a movie on YouTube that's full of 80' neon and music. I love the 80's and so I decided to try my hand at some color. I HATE using color since I don't have much practice with it, and I prefer black and white drawings anyways.

Digital Art Attempt

Lastly, this is my attempt at digital art! I've always been intrigued by it and really wanted to see what it was all about. It's a lot harder than I thought it would be. I'm so used to actually feeling the paper and pencil on each other. This one took me a day to finish, and I love it. Going back to Disney, "The Goofy Movie" and "An Extremely Goofy Movie" were some of my favorites to watch growing up! So that was really my inspiration for This drawing. One thing I do love about digital art though is the rewind feature, so much better than an actual eraser!
